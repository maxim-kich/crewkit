How it works

The rationale behind CrewKit and how to develop it further.

The Core Idea

CrewKit is built on a simple principle: define once, use everywhere. It's a single source of truth for your team structure and evaluation criteria.

Everything lives in a single setup.json file that you own, control, and can share selectively. No backend, no accounts, no data leaving your machine. Just you, your team, and the data you choose to keep.

Your setup.json: The Foundation

Think of setup.json as your team's operating manual. It contains everything:

  • Your company configuration and team members
  • Skill matrices and evaluation templates
  • Assessment criteria and scoring grids
  • Your team member data and progress

When you load it into the app, everything is restored exactly as you left it.

Keep a backup. Download your setup.json regularly using the button in the header. The app saves to your browser's localStorage, which persists as long as you don't clear your data—but why risk it?

Getting Started: Module Build Order

Start with the foundation. Everything else depends on it.

1. Artifacts First Create your evaluation templates. Skill matrices, scoring grids, questionnaires—these define your assessment criteria and structure. Think of artifacts as the rules of the game. Build them once, reuse them everywhere.

2. Then Add Crew Members Bring your team into the system. Evaluate each person against the artifacts you created. Track skills, proficiency levels, and progress over time. This is where your team's growth becomes visible.

3. Finally, Organize Initiatives Group your projects, goals, and team efforts. Track updates and ownership.

Follow this order and you'll have a solid foundation for everything that comes next.

Share Individual Components

You don't always need to share the entire system. CrewKit lets you share specific pieces:

  • Artifacts alone: Send a skill matrix or questionnaire to a colleague. They can evaluate themselves or others without needing your full setup.json.
  • Crew profiles: Download individual team member profiles and share results privately.
  • The full setup.json: Back up everything or hand off the entire system to another account.

Each file is self-contained. Load any of them into another CrewKit instance and they work independently.

Fill Out Mode: Lightweight Sharing

Sometimes your team members just need to fill out an evaluation—they don't need the entire app.

They can upload a standalone artifact file and fill it out. Let them complete the evaluation without needing setup.json. They download their results as a separate file to share back with you. Simple, no friction, no onboarding.

Extending CrewKit

CrewKit is open source and built with vanilla JavaScript. No build tools, no dependencies—just HTML, CSS, and JavaScript you can read and modify directly.

Fork it, experiment with it, make it yours.

If you build something cool the door is open:

  • Repository: github.com/maxim-kich/crewkit
  • Create a pull request and share your ideas
  • Open an issue to discuss potential improvements
  • Write directly to the author with suggestions and feedback

CrewKit is meant to evolve with the needs of design teams. Your contributions are welcome.
